Hi, I'm Sarwar and I currently work as a Research Associate within the Biostatistics Research Group which is based in the Department of Health Sciences at the University of Leicester, UK.

My main research interests lie in survival analysis, particulary in competing risks theory and the analysis of cancer registry data. I also enjoy programming (I mean, who doesn't, right?), mainly in Stata and have sort of, by accident, become a self-taught programmer in JavaScript/HTML/CSS. In particular, I use d3.js for the development of interactive tools to communicate complex statistical models.

I recently completed my PhD thesis titled, "Development of Flexible Parametric Models for Competing Risks and Tools to Facilitate in the Understanding and Communication of Cancer Survival", also at the University of Leicester, under the supervision of Prof. Paul C. Lambert and Dr. Mark J. Rutherford. As a part of this PhD, I developed the online interactive tool, InterPreT Cancer Survival, which piqued my interest in web development. Further details on the research carried out during my PhD can be found here.

As well as to disseminate research (software, talks and the sort...), this website was also created as a platform for releasing some interactive visualisation projects I'll be working on. Bear in mind, I have just begun, so at the moment it is pretty empty, but expect content to be added soon!
